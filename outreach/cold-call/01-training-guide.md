# Corgi Cold Call — Training Guide

## 1. The Mental Model (know this cold)

**What Corgi actually does:** Provides a residual value floor on data-center GPUs — so lenders feel confident the collateral will hold value at maturity (3-5 years out). This isn't insurance you're selling. It's a financing enabler.

**The core insight:** GPU demand is exploding, but lenders still hesitate because no one knows what an H100 is worth in 2029. Corgi removes that uncertainty from the equation. The result: cheaper debt, higher leverage, longer terms.

**Why this matters to each persona:**

| Persona | They care about… | So you lead with… |
|---|---|---|
| GPU operator / data center | Getting more hardware financed at better terms | "Cheaper, cleaner financing" |
| Lender / credit fund | Deploying capital into AI infra without residual value anxiety | "Make more GPU-backed loans with downside protection" |
| Structured capital / arranger | Closing deals faster, placing debt with more lenders | "Residual value floor that makes the package easier to place" |

---

## 2. The Language Rules

### Always lead with capital efficiency, never insurance.
The product may technically involve insurance structures, but the *buyer* doesn't care about that. They care about: **Will this make my financing cheaper / possible / faster?**

### Vocabulary discipline

✅ **Use freely:** cost of capital · debt structure · collateral · downside protection · underwriting confidence · residual value floor

🚫 **Don't say (unless they ask):** reinsurance · Cayman · Lloyd's · captive · policy wording · put option

**Why this matters:** The moment you say "reinsurance" or "policy," the prospect mentally recategorises you as an insurance salesperson. You lose the capital-markets frame. Stay in their world.

---

## 3. Script Selection Guide

You have 5 scripts. Here's when to use each:

| Script | Best for | Opening energy | CTA style |
|---|---|---|---|
| **A – Cheaper Capital** | Operators actively financing GPUs | Direct, benefit-first | Soft ("would it be useful to show you…") |
| **B – Better Debt Structure** | Operators who've dealt with lender pushback | Problem-aware, empathetic | Direct ("would next week work?") |
| **C – Lender Angle** | Credit funds, equipment finance teams | Peer-to-peer, professional | Consultative ("fit your credit box?") |
| **D – Operator Pain** | Data centers feeling pricing pain on debt | Empathetic, specific | Founder intro ask |
| **E – Simple CTA** | Anyone, especially cold-cold outreach | Minimal, low-pressure | Permission-based ("is 20 mins worth it?") |

**Rule of thumb:** If in doubt, use **E**. It's the lowest-commitment opener and works with anyone.

---

## 4. The Call Structure (all scripts follow this)

```
1. ONE-LINER       → Who you are and what Corgi does (1 sentence)
2. RELEVANCE HOOK  → Why this matters to *them* (1 sentence)  
3. BRIDGE          → How it works at a high level (1-2 sentences)
4. CTA             → Book 20 mins with Isaac and Josh
```

**That's it.** The entire first call should be 2-3 minutes. You're not closing. You're not explaining policy structure. You're getting a meeting.

---

## 5. Handling "What exactly is this?"

This will come up. The playbook gives you a clean 4-line response:

1. "It's a residual value solution for data-center GPUs."
2. "The simple purpose is to reduce lender downside on future hardware value."
3. "That can help make debt cheaper or easier to raise."
4. "On the next call, Isaac and Josh can walk through how it works in the financing stack."

**Key discipline:** Line 4 is the exit. Don't get pulled into explaining mechanics. The founders handle that.

---

## 6. Follow-Up Questions (use to qualify + build rapport)

These aren't random — each one tells you something:

| Question | What it reveals |
|---|---|
| "Are you financing owned GPUs, leased GPUs, or both?" | Ownership structure — Corgi is most relevant for owned hardware |
| "Who leads those conversations: treasury, infra, CFO, or financing partner?" | Whether you're talking to the right person or need a warm intro |
| "Do lenders push back more on leverage, tenor, or pricing?" | Where the pain is sharpest — tailor your follow-up accordingly |
| "Do you already have a lender asking about collateral value at maturity?" | If yes, this is a hot lead — the problem is already live |
| "Would you look at this for the next cluster only, or as a standard financing tool?" | Signals deal size and strategic intent |

---

## 7. Qualification Checklist (before investing time)

Before a second call, mentally check:

- [ ] Do they **own or finance** GPUs directly (not just resell cloud)?
- [ ] Is there a **live or upcoming debt process** in the next 6-12 months?
- [ ] Would better cost of capital, longer tenor, or higher leverage **actually matter** to them?
- [ ] Is there a clear **lender, credit fund, or treasury contact** behind the build?
- [ ] Can they plausibly hold or finance **$50M-$150M+** of GPU hardware over time?

3+ checks = worth a founder call. Fewer = keep warm but don't over-invest.

## 8. Common Objections & How to Handle

| Objection | What they're really saying | Response framework |
|---|---|---|
| "We're not raising debt right now" | Timing isn't right, but might be later | "Understood — when you do, this can be set up in advance. Mind if I check back in Q3?" |
| "Our lenders haven't raised this concern" | They might not know what they don't know | "That's actually common — it usually surfaces when the lender's IC looks at maturity assumptions. Worth having in your back pocket." |
| "Sounds like insurance" | You've lost the capital-efficiency frame | "It has an insurance backbone, but the purpose is financing — it's designed to sit inside a debt structure, not alongside it." |
| "We lease, we don't own" | Might not be a fit | "Got it — Corgi is most relevant for owned hardware today. If that changes, we'd love to talk." |
| "What's the cost?" | Premature but legitimate | "It depends on the hardware profile and tenor. Isaac can walk through indicative pricing on a quick call — it's designed to be NPV-positive against the financing improvement." |
| "Send me something" | Polite brush-off OR genuine interest | "Happy to — I'll send a one-pager. Can I also suggest 20 minutes with Isaac? The numbers make more sense in context." |
| "I need to talk to my CFO / treasury" | Good sign — means they see relevance | "That makes sense. Would it help if I set up the call with them included?" |

---

## 9. Mindset Rules

1. **You are not selling insurance.** You are helping them get a better debt structure.
2. **The first call sells the meeting, not the product.** If you're explaining policy mechanics, you've gone too far.
3. **Curiosity > persuasion.** Ask questions. Learn their situation. The best cold calls feel like a conversation, not a pitch.
4. **Silence is information.** If they go quiet after your opener, wait. Don't fill the gap with more features.
5. **"Not now" ≠ "No."** Most of these companies will finance GPUs eventually. Plant the seed. Follow up.
