# Corgi Cold Call — Practice Scenarios

## Scenario 1: The Warm Operator
**Company:** Voltage Park (Priority A operator)  
**Who you reach:** VP of Infrastructure Finance  
**Situation:** They're actively looking at debt to finance their next GPU cluster. They've had preliminary lender conversations but haven't closed anything yet. They're receptive but time-poor.  
**Best script to try:** A or B  
**Difficulty:** ⭐⭐ (Easy-Medium)  
**What to practice:** Clean opener, reading buying signals, getting a firm meeting time  
**Curveball:** They'll ask "what's the cost?" early in the call  

---

## Scenario 2: The Skeptical Lender
**Company:** Victory Park Capital (Priority B lender)  
**Who you reach:** Managing Director, credit origination  
**Situation:** They've looked at GPU-backed deals before but passed because residual value made them uncomfortable. They're smart, direct, and will push back on anything that sounds like a pitch.  
**Best script to try:** C  
**Difficulty:** ⭐⭐⭐ (Medium)  
**What to practice:** Lender-specific language, staying in the capital-efficiency frame, handling "sounds like insurance"  
**Curveball:** They'll say "We looked at this space and the residual value problem is why we passed. How is your solution any different?"  

---

## Scenario 3: The Gatekeeper
**Company:** Genesis Cloud (Priority A operator)  
**Who you reach:** Head of Operations (not the financing decision-maker)  
**Situation:** You wanted the CFO but got routed here. This person is polite but doesn't fully understand their own company's financing structure. They can refer you up, but you need to earn it.  
**Best script to try:** D or E  
**Difficulty:** ⭐⭐⭐ (Medium)  
**What to practice:** Simplifying the message, navigating to the right person without being pushy, leaving a hook  
**Curveball:** "I'm not sure this is relevant for us — we handle our infrastructure differently"  

---

## Scenario 4: The "Send Me Something" Brush-Off
**Company:** CUDO Compute (Priority B operator)  
**Who you reach:** Co-founder  
**Situation:** They're building GPU capacity but aren't actively financing. The co-founder is busy, half-listening, and will try to end the call politely with "just send me an email."  
**Best script to try:** E  
**Difficulty:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Hard)  
**What to practice:** Recovering from a brush-off, creating urgency without pressure, earning 30 more seconds  
**Curveball:** "Look, I've got a lot going on — can you just send me something and I'll take a look?"  

---

## Scenario 5: The Deep Diver
**Company:** Upper90 (Priority B lender)  
**Who you reach:** Principal, credit team  
**Situation:** They led the Crusoe GPU credit facility. They understand this space deeply and will ask pointed technical questions about how Corgi's product actually works — coverage terms, who the counterparty is, how claims work.  
**Best script to try:** C  
**Difficulty:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Hard)  
**What to practice:** Handling deep technical questions without over-explaining, knowing when to defer to the founders, staying in your lane  
**Curveball:** "Walk me through the actual coverage structure. Who's the risk carrier? What triggers a payout?"  

---

## Scenario 6: The "We Lease Everything" Dead End
**Company:** Hypothetical mid-market operator  
**Who you reach:** VP of Infrastructure  
**Situation:** Turns out they lease all their GPUs and don't carry residual value risk. This isn't a fit right now.  
**Best script to try:** Any  
**Difficulty:** ⭐⭐ (Easy)  
**What to practice:** Graceful disqualification, leaving the door open, asking if they know anyone who owns  
**Curveball:** None — the whole point is recognising the dead end and exiting cleanly

## Scenario 7: The Warm Intro Follow-Up
**Company:** Verda / DataCrunch (Priority A operator)  
**Who you reach:** CFO  
**Situation:** Someone in your network introduced you. The CFO took the call as a favour. They're polite, somewhat interested, but need to be convinced this is worth 20 more minutes. They already use debt (Nordea, Armada Credit Partners) so they understand the financing landscape.  
**Best script to try:** B  
**Difficulty:** ⭐⭐⭐ (Medium)  
**What to practice:** Leveraging existing debt context, being credible with someone who knows the space, earning the founder meeting  
**Curveball:** "We already have competitive terms with our lenders. Why would we need this?"  

---

## Scenario 8: The European Sovereign AI Angle
**Company:** Ori (Priority B operator/platform)  
**Who you reach:** Head of Partnerships  
**Situation:** They're an orchestration platform enabling sovereign AI infrastructure for telcos. They don't directly own GPUs but enable deployments. The value prop is indirect — Corgi could be a financing layer that makes their partners' GPU purchases easier.  
**Best script to try:** D (adapted)  
**Difficulty:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Hard)  
**What to practice:** Adapting the message for an indirect buyer, understanding where Corgi fits in an ecosystem play, creative qualification  
**Curveball:** "We don't own the hardware. Our partners do. But I can see how this might be interesting for them."

## How to Use These

1. **Pick a scenario** — start with 1 or 2 if you're warming up
2. **Tell me which script you want to practise** (or say "you pick")
3. **I'll play the prospect** — in character, with realistic responses
4. **After the call, I'll give you a debrief:** what worked, what didn't, specific line-by-line feedback
5. **Run it again** if you want — I'll vary the responses so it's not identical

### Scoring (I'll rate each attempt)

- **Opener clarity** — Did they immediately understand what you do? (1-5)
- **Frame control** — Did you stay in capital-efficiency, not insurance? (1-5)
- **Qualification** — Did you learn what you needed to? (1-5)
- **CTA execution** — Did you get the meeting or a clear next step? (1-5)
- **Objection handling** — How did you handle pushback? (1-5)
- **Overall flow** — Did it feel like a conversation or a script read? (1-5)
