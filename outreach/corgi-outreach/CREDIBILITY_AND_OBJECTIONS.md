## SECTION 1: WHAT MAKES A NEW INSURANCE PRODUCT TRUSTED BY INSTITUTIONAL LENDERS

### The Five Trust Pillars

**1. Reinsurance Backing**
The single most important credibility signal. Lenders don't care who the fronting insurer is — they care who reinsures the risk. A named, rated reinsurer (e.g., Swiss Re, Munich Re, Hannover Re, Berkshire Hathaway Re) is the difference between "interesting startup" and "institutional-grade product."

*How to deploy this:* Name the reinsurer in every conversation. "We're backed by [reinsurer name], who has A+ financial strength rating from AM Best."

**2. Financial Strength Ratings**
AM Best rating (A- or better) signals institutional viability. Lenders' internal credit committees require rated counterparties for any insurance product used as credit enhancement. S&P/Fitch ratings add additional weight.

*For a new entrant:* The path is often: captive/MGA structure → Lloyd's syndicate capacity → rated reinsurer treaty → eventual direct rated entity.

**3. Claims-Paying Ability / Trust Account**
Some lenders require a funded trust account or letter of credit from the insurer — demonstrating actual claims-paying capacity independent of the company's operating model.

**4. Legal & Regulatory Standing**
- Licensed in the relevant jurisdiction (Lloyd's coverholder, Bermuda-domiciled excess & surplus)
- Policy wording reviewed by lender's legal counsel (major firms: Vedder Price, Chapman and Cutler, Otterbourg)
- NAIC recognition: RVI is explicitly recognized by the NAIC as an acceptable risk mitigation tool in credit tenant lease financings

**5. Track Record / Analogous Precedent**
Lenders ask: "Has this product worked before?" The answer for GPU RVG is found in:
- Aviation RVI (35+ year track record)
- Marine vessel RVI
- Commercial equipment RVI (construction, medical, tech)
- The RVI Group alone has insured $59B+ in residuals since 1989

## SECTION 2: THE AVIATION RVI ANALOG — ASSURED GUARANTY RE OVERSEAS (AGRO)

### How AGRO Built Credibility in Aircraft RVI

AGRO (Assured Guaranty Re Overseas Ltd., Bermuda) is the closest institutional analog to what Corgi is building for GPUs.

**Their Positioning:**
- Specialized in commercial aircraft RVI
- Offered both **direct** coverage to institutional investors AND **reinsurance** to other RVI providers
- Targeted: *"modern, in-production, in-demand aircraft with deep operator bases and high mission flexibility"*
- This is almost verbatim how GPU RVG should be pitched: "modern, in-production, in-demand NVIDIA GPUs with deep operator bases and high utilization rates"

**Their Credibility Architecture:**
1. Backed by Assured Guaranty Group (world's largest financial guaranty reinsurer)
2. AM Best: A+ (Superior) Financial Strength Rating, "aa" Long-Term Issuer Credit Rating
3. Low competition: "minimal number of players" in this niche
4. Aggregate cover AND individual line size available
5. Profitable underwriting track record (negative loss ratio = favorable reserve development)

**What Happened to Aircraft RVI:**
- Suspended since COVID-19 (2020) — planes stopped flying, values collapsed catastrophically
- Key lesson: asset selection matters. Aircraft RVI worked for decades on planes with stable demand, then failed on a black swan (COVID). GPUs face tech risk, not demand destruction — different risk profile.

**The Pitch for Corgi:**
*"AGRO built aircraft RVI credibility through institutional backing, rigorous asset selection (modern/in-demand planes), and actuarial pricing. We're doing the same for GPUs — but with a key advantage: SiliconNavigator provides real-time secondary market data that AGRO never had for aircraft."*

## SECTION 3: EQUIPMENT RVI PRECEDENTS

### Aviation (Strongest Precedent)
- **RVI Group** (Charlotte, NC): $59B insured since 1989; $45B+ matured positions; profitable track record
- **AGRO**: Aircraft RVI before COVID suspension
- **AerCap / ORIX Aviation**: Embedded RVI structures in aircraft leasing

**Lesson:** Aviation RVI normalized when institutional lessors (GE Capital, ILFC, AerCap) used it as a credit enhancement to access capital markets. First mover advantage was real.

### Commercial Equipment
- **Matrix Specialty Underwriting**: Writes RVI for construction, industrial, medical, commercial vehicles
- **Tokio Marine HCC**: Writes Residual Value Guaranty product for commercial equipment
- **Asset RVI LLC**: Specialty underwriter for equipment RVI

**Lesson:** Once the product class existed in aviation, equipment finance adopted it. GPU RVG follows this same pattern.

### Technology Equipment
- **Computer/tech equipment RVI has historically been difficult** due to rapid depreciation
- Some specialized RVI providers avoided tech; others wrote it with strict asset selection (enterprise servers from Tier 1 OEMs)
- **Key precedent:** The $1.1B GPU ABS deal (early 2025, AAA-rated) — rating agencies accepted GPU collateral as investment-grade when properly structured. This validates the actuarial case for GPU RVG.

## SECTION 4: TOP 10 LENDER OBJECTIONS & KILLER RESPONSES

---

### OBJECTION 1: "You're too new. You haven't paid a claim."

**Response:**
*"Fair point — and we'd expect nothing less from a credit-focused institution. Here's the honest answer: every RVI product started with its first deal. AGRO wrote its first aircraft policy. RVI Group wrote its first equipment policy. The question isn't whether we're new — it's whether our risk model is sound and our backing is real. Our reinsurance partner is [name], with A+ AM Best rating and capacity for this product. And we're not writing this based on guesswork — we're pricing it off SiliconNavigator's real-time GPU secondary market data. Would it help if we walked your credit committee through the actuarial model?"*

---

### OBJECTION 2: "You're not rated."

**Response:**
*"Correct, as a new entity we don't yet have a direct AM Best rating. But our reinsurance treaty is with [rated reinsurer], and the policy structure means your claim is ultimately against that rated balance sheet — not ours. Many Lloyd's coverholders and MGAs operate this way — the credit quality is the capacity provider, not the intermediary. We're happy to share the reinsurance treaty structure for your legal review."*

---

### OBJECTION 3: "I'll wait for an established insurer to offer this."

**Response:**
*"I'd bet my career you'll wait at least 3-5 years. Here's why: Munich Re, Swiss Re, and Allianz all have tech equipment underwriting teams that are still building GPU depreciation models from scratch. The established insurers are slow by design. Meanwhile, you're writing GPU deals today — deals where depreciation risk is real and present. The first lenders to use RVG will write more deals, at better terms, than those who wait. First-mover advantage in equipment finance is very real — ask anyone who started using equipment ABS in the early 90s."*

---

### OBJECTION 4: "GPUs depreciate too fast for insurance to work."

**Response:**
*"Actually, that's precisely why insurance works here — the risk is quantifiable. We use SiliconNavigator's real-time secondary market data — the same data the GPU ABS market used to achieve AAA ratings in early 2025. We can price a depreciation curve for H100/H200/Blackwell with statistical confidence. The concern isn't that GPUs depreciate — it's that the rate is unpredictable. Our actuarial model accounts for that uncertainty with appropriate margin, which is exactly what insurance pricing does. What's the current advance rate you're applying to Blackwell collateral?"*

---

### OBJECTION 5: "What if a new NVIDIA GPU launches and kills the value of the collateral overnight?"

**Response:**
*"That's the tech cycle risk, and it's exactly what we price for. Our model uses product lifecycle data — release dates, gen-over-gen performance jumps, secondary market liquidity — to assess how much price impact a new GPU launch typically creates. The Blackwell launch's impact on H100 prices was significant but not catastrophic, and our stress scenarios go beyond observed history. We also apply exclusions for end-of-life products, which is why we only cover in-production, in-demand architectures — the same approach AGRO used for modern aircraft."*

---

### OBJECTION 6: "The borrower won't pay for this."

**Response:**
*"Two things: First, the borrower often does pay when the value proposition is clear — it's the price of getting a better deal. If RVG allows you to advance 75% vs 60% LTV on the same collateral, the borrower's net cost of capital goes down even after paying the premium. Second, the premium can be structured into the loan economics — it doesn't have to be a separate line item. We can model this for your specific deal structure."*

---

### OBJECTION 7: "We already have internal depreciation models."

**Response:**
*"Great — so you already understand the risk. The question is whether an internal model creates contractual certainty, or just a best estimate. If GPU prices fall 40% and you foreclose, your model doesn't pay your investor — the collateral value does. RVG converts your model's estimate into a contractual commitment. It's the difference between your credit memo saying 'we believe recovery will be X' and your credit memo saying 'recovery at maturity is contractually guaranteed to be no less than X.' For an institutional LP base, that's a fundamentally different credit quality."*

---

### OBJECTION 8: "This is too complicated for our deals."

**Response:**
*"First deal typically takes 2-3 weeks of documentation review and policy issuance. After that, repeat transactions are typically 2-week turnaround. For deals of $10M+, that's well within your normal underwriting timeline. And our policy structure is designed to integrate cleanly with standard equipment loan documentation — we work with equipment finance counsel who can speak directly with your legal team."*

---

### OBJECTION 9: "What if Corgi goes out of business before the loan matures?"

**Response:**
*"Good question — that's exactly why the reinsurance structure matters. The policy is backed by [reinsurer], and we'd typically structure a trust account or assignment of rights as additional assurance. Even if Corgi ceased to operate, the reinsurance treaty would be triggered directly. We can structure policy assignment to the lender as additional credit support."*

---

### OBJECTION 10: "The GPU market is too illiquid for a floor price to be meaningful."

**Response:**
*"GPU secondary markets are more liquid than most equipment categories — there are active online marketplaces (eBay, BackBlaze sales, secondary GPU brokers) and institutional trade desks. SiliconNavigator specifically tracks this. Compare to aircraft, where secondary market values required specialized brokers and AVAC appraisals — GPUs have deeper, faster, more transparent secondary markets. The floor is priced based on actual traded values, not theoretical estimates."*
