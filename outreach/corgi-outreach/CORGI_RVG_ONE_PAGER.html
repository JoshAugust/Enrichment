The Problem

GPU depreciation is the unhedged risk on every AI infrastructure loan.

Lenders face structural uncertainty: AI hardware depreciates rapidly, secondary market liquidity is thin, and model generations shift values overnight. Standard loan structures leave that tail risk entirely on the balance sheet.

18–36mo

Generational Cycles Compress Value

NVIDIA releases new datacenter GPU architectures every 12–18 months. Prior-gen assets face step-change devaluation as compute buyers migrate to next-gen silicon — compressing residual values faster than loan amortisation schedules.

↓LTV

Conservative Advance Rates Constrain Deployment

Without residual value protection, lenders apply aggressive haircuts — often 50–65% LTV — to account for depreciation risk. This leaves borrowers undercapitalised and lenders losing deals to competitors willing to take more risk.

$0

No Instrument Has Existed to Transfer This Risk

Until now, GPU lenders had no mechanism to offload collateral depreciation risk. Aviation solved this problem in 1989. The structured credit, insurance, and capital markets infrastructure for GPU assets is only beginning to form.

The Solution

A contractual floor on GPU collateral value — at the date that matters.

The Corgi RVG is an insurance policy issued to the lender (or SPV) that guarantees a minimum residual value for covered GPU assets at a specified future date, aligned to loan maturity.

If the prevailing secondary market value of the covered hardware falls below the guaranteed floor, Corgi pays the delta. If the market holds, the policy simply expires. The borrower pays the premium; the lender holds the protection.

Pricing is actuarially derived using SiliconNavigator real-time GPU secondary market data — the most granular GPU pricing intelligence available — combined with hardware depreciation curves, supply-demand modelling, and fleet-level utilisation data.

All capacity is reinsurance-backed, ensuring counterparty strength consistent with rated institutional counterparties.

Regulatory Recognition
The National Association of Insurance Commissioners recognises Residual Value Insurance as an acceptable risk mitigation instrument. The regulatory framework exists — the product just needed to be applied to this asset class.

Aviation lenders faced an identical problem in the 1980s. Jet aircraft are high-value, depreciating assets with volatile secondary markets and rapid technological obsolescence. Residual Value Insurance emerged as the instrument to bridge that gap — enabling longer loans, better advance rates, and a functional secondary market for aviation credit.

GPU infrastructure is the jet aircraft of the AI era. The depreciation dynamics are faster, the market is less liquid, and the capital demand is growing faster than any aviation cycle in history.

Corgi is bringing the same proven structure to the asset class defining the next decade of infrastructure investment — before the market settles on a standard. The lenders who build RVG into their GPU underwriting now will define that standard.

Regulatory Standing

The NAIC formally recognises Residual Value Insurance as an acceptable credit risk mitigation instrument. Corgi's RVG policies are structured to qualify under this framework, supporting potential regulatory capital treatment for covered exposures.
