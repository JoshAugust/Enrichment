# 🎯 Corgi Outreach — Revised Game Plan

## What We're Actually Selling

**GPU Residual Value Guaranty (RVG)** — an insurance policy that guarantees a floor on GPU hardware value at a future date. If GPUs depreciate below the floor, the policy pays out and takes title.

**The killer value prop:** This insurance **lowers the cost of capital** for debt-financed GPU companies. When a lender knows the collateral has a guaranteed floor, they offer better rates, longer tenors, and higher LTV ratios.

## Three Target Segments

### 1. GPU Data Centers / Operators (Direct Policyholders)
**Who:** Mid-size companies that OWN GPU hardware, ideally debt-financed.
**Why they buy:** RVG lowers their cost of capital by de-risking their lenders' collateral.
**Sweet spot:** $50M-$500M in GPU hardware, NOT mega-operators like CoreWeave.

### 2. Lenders (Mandate the Policy)
**Who:** Private credit funds and equipment finance firms doing GPU-backed lending.
**Why they care:** RVG provides a guaranteed collateral floor → better LTV → reduced credit risk.
**Note from supervisor:** "Lenders — not like CoreWeave, too big" — target lenders serving mid-market borrowers, not the mega-facilities.

### 3. Cayman Island Reinsurance Companies (Provide Capacity)
**Who:** 113 licensed reinsurers in Cayman (from 58 in 2020 to 113 in 2025). $25B written premiums, $101B AUM.
**Why Cayman:** "Easy to go direct" — less intermediation than Lloyd's or Bermuda. Faster decisions.
**Key:** Cayman P&C reinsurers, NOT the life/annuity ones that dominate Cayman.
