## KEY INSIGHT: WHICH LENDER CATEGORY IS MOST PROMISING?

### 🥇 Investment Banks (Wall Street) — HIGHEST MULTIPLIER

The CoreWeave deals revealed something extraordinary: **9 major Wall Street banks are ALREADY doing GPU-backed loans** and none of them have RVG coverage. Each bank has multiple borrowers on their books. One RVG mandate from Morgan Stanley or SocGen = potentially dozens of automatic Corgi customers.

**But:** Long sales cycles, procurement process, legal review. Relationship-driven.

**Strategy:** Target the deal-origination MDs directly (like Valtin Gallani at SocGen). Position RVG as a competitive deal-structuring tool, not a product sale.

### 🥈 Specialist Private Credit (IDF, LuminArx) — FASTEST CONVERSIONS

These boutique firms invented GPU-backed lending. They understand the collateral risk better than anyone. Small teams = faster decisions. No procurement bureaucracy. Direct access to decision makers.

**IDF** in particular has a CEO with a published email and a stated mission around GPU infrastructure. This is the easiest conversation to start.

### 🥉 Equipment Finance Companies — LONGEST RUNWAY BUT BIGGEST VOLUME

Ascentium Capital, CSC Leasing, Navitas etc. do hundreds of tech equipment deals per year. If one mandates RVG across their GPU portfolio, the policy volume is enormous. But they need education — they haven't thought about GPU residual value as a distinct insurance product yet. 

**Pitch angle:** "You finance servers and IT equipment. GPUs are the new category and they depreciate 40-60% per year. RVG is the product that lets you write those loans without worrying about residual value."
