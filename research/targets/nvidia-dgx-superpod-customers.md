## 🎓 University / Research Deployments

### University of Florida (HiPerGator AI 4.0)
- **System:** DGX SuperPOD with DGX B200 Systems
- **Cluster Size:** 63 DGX B200 systems → 504 NVIDIA Blackwell GPUs; NVIDIA Quantum-2 InfiniBand (400 Gb/s per GPU); NVIDIA AI Enterprise software
- **Use Case:** Academic AI research across all disciplines; the "AI University" mission; ~7,000 researchers from across the Southeast; 33M+ research requests/year
- **Announcement Date:** December 2024 (Board approval); components arrived January 2025; full production Fall 2025; unveiling October 2025
- **Notes:** First university to adopt DGX SuperPOD with Blackwell; $24–33M investment; 30x faster than previous HiPerGator generation; one of the fastest academic supercomputers in higher education
- **Key Contacts:** Not publicly disclosed
- **Source:** [UF news](https://news.ufl.edu/2024/12/hipergator-upgrade/), [UF unveiling](https://news.ufl.edu/2025/10/hipergator-4-unveiling/)

---

### Linköping University / National Supercomputer Centre (Berzelius)
- **System:** DGX SuperPOD with DGX A100 (initial) + DGX H200 (expansion)
- **Cluster Size:** Initial: 60 DGX A100 → 300 petaFLOPS; Expansion 2023: +34 DGX A100 → 94 nodes total, 470 petaFLOPS (~half exaFLOP); Latest expansion 2024–25: +16 DGX H200 nodes
- **Use Case:** National AI research infrastructure for Sweden; NLP (GPT-SW3 Swedish LLM), materials science, bioinformatics, life sciences, quantum technology
- **Announcement Date:** 2021 (initial); January 2023 (expansion); 2024 (H200 expansion)
- **Notes:** Named after chemist Jacob Berzelius; funded by 300M SEK (~$33.6M) Knut and Alice Wallenberg Foundation donation; built by Atos; ranked among world's 100 fastest AI supercomputers post-2023 expansion
- **Key Contacts:** Thomas Richert (NSC Director); Knut and Alice Wallenberg Foundation (funder)
- **Source:** [NVIDIA blog](https://blogs.nvidia.com/blog/linkoeping-university-ai-supercomputer/), [NSC expansion](https://www.nsc.liu.se/2023/01/24/Berzelius-expansion.html)

## 🔍 Research Notes

- **Fortune 100 adoption**: Nvidia claims over half of Fortune 100 companies use DGX systems (on-prem or cloud), but most are not named publicly
- **Largest clusters**: The hyperscalers (Microsoft, Google, Oracle, AWS, Meta) consume the most GPUs but primarily through Blackwell H100/GB200 infrastructure, not always branded as "DGX SuperPOD"
- **Planned Blackwell adopters** (publicly named by Nvidia): AWS, Dell Technologies, Google, Meta, Microsoft, OpenAI, Oracle, Tesla, xAI
- **All Blackwell GPU production through 2025 has been pre-allocated** to major cloud customers
- **DGX Cloud pricing**: ~$36,999/instance/month; includes NVIDIA AI Enterprise software suite
- **Next-gen platform**: NVIDIA DGX SuperPOD Rubin (successor to Blackwell) announced for future deployments
