# Lloyd's of London: Specific Syndicates for GPU RVG Underwriting

*Research compiled 29 March 2026*

## Executive Summary

Lloyd's of London is the natural home for GPU Residual Value Guarantee (RVG) policies. With 84 active syndicates, 8 special purpose arrangements, and 9 syndicates-in-a-box (as of end 2024), the market wrote £55.5bn in gross premiums. Several syndicates already write technology equipment, data center, and innovative specialty lines that map directly to GPU RVG risk. Lloyd's also has explicit innovation pathways (coverholder model, Syndicate-in-a-Box, Innovation ICX class) that could enable Corgi to bring GPU RVG to market.

## 2. Most Innovative / Novel Product Syndicates

### Munich Re Innovation Syndicate 1840 (Now in Run-off)
- **First-ever Syndicate-in-a-Box** at Lloyd's (launched January 2020)
- Designed as a "fail-fast" innovation lab / incubator for products that don't fit traditional lines
- Focus areas: weather parametric products, green energy equipment failure coverage, hobby drones, autonomous vehicles, city scooter hire insurance
- **Key insight:** The green energy product covered the risk of equipment failure leaving investors with debt — **directly analogous to GPU RVG** (equipment depreciation risk leaving lessors/financiers exposed)
- Now in run-off, but proved the SiaB model works for novel risk classes
- Active underwriter Stuart Newcombe moved to NormanMax 3939

### ATA (Advanced Technology Assurance) — $750M AI Infrastructure Facility (Jan 2026)
- **Most directly relevant precedent for GPU RVG**
- London-based MGU launched integrated, modular policy for AI infrastructure
- Backed by 10+ (re)insurers and Lloyd's syndicates including Arch, Munich Re Specialty, SCOR
- Covers: property, **computer hardware**, cargo & transit, cyber, tech E&O, environmental, terrorism — **under one policy**
- Previously, hyperscale developers had to patch together standalone policies with coverage gaps
- Rachel Turk (Lloyd's Chief of Market Performance) endorsed as demonstrating Lloyd's syndication model
- **Target market:** "Blue-chip" investors, developers, tenants, and **chip providers** driving AI build-out

### Lockton + Parametrix — Parametric SLA Insurance
- Backed by Lloyd's syndicates
- Parametric coverage for **data center performance risk** (SLA guarantees)
- Addresses financial protection against downtime — related to equipment performance guarantees

### Beazley Syndicate 4321 (SiaB)
- Lloyd's syndicate-in-a-box focused on consortium business based on **ESG scores of insureds**
- Demonstrates willingness to create novel underwriting criteria

### Lloyd's Innovation ICX & Transition TCX Classes (May 2024)
- **Innovation ICX:** Syndicates can write up to **additional 5% of GWP** for innovative business, without impacting their performance oversight metrics
- **Transition TCX:** Same 5% allowance for transition/sustainability risks
- **Critical for GPU RVG:** Any syndicate could write GPU RVG under the Innovation ICX class without it counting against their BAU performance metrics
- Lloyd's explicitly states: *"For truly new risks Lloyd's does not expect a track record but expects Managing Agents to have carried out a proportionate degree of analysis"*
- *"Small experiments may be needed to establish data when none exists"*

## 3. Lloyd's Coverholder / MGA Model — Could Corgi Become a Coverholder?

### What Is a Coverholder?
A coverholder is an entity authorised by a Lloyd's managing agent to enter into contracts of insurance on behalf of the syndicate. This is the **MGA (Managing General Agent)** model — the coverholder underwrites and binds policies, with the syndicate providing capacity (capital).

### Why This Is the Path for Corgi
- Corgi wouldn't need its own syndicate or capital — it would act as the **underwriting brain** while a syndicate provides capacity
- There are currently **381 registered brokers** and thousands of coverholders globally
- **Fast-track process introduced 2024:** Approval reduced from months to **as little as 2 weeks** once application is complete
- Lloyd's has delegated final approval authority to managing agents to reduce bureaucracy

### Requirements to Become a Lloyd's Coverholder

| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| **Sponsorship** | Must be sponsored by a Lloyd's Broker AND a Managing Agent (syndicate) |
| **Business plan** | Clear underwriting strategy, target market, projected GWP |
| **Systems** | Robust policy management system; must pass a **live demo** to Lloyd's |
| **Staff** | Experienced key personnel with relevant underwriting expertise |
| **E&O Insurance** | Professional indemnity / errors & omissions coverage required |
| **Compliance** | Compliant money handling; sign Coverholder Undertaking |
| **Managing Agent attestation** | Sponsoring syndicate signs off that coverholder meets all requirements |
| **Country Manager review** | Lloyd's Country Manager in relevant territory reviews application |
| **Platform** | Application submitted through **ATLAS** (Lloyd's electronic filing system) |

### The Corgi Coverholder Playbook
1. **Find a sponsoring syndicate** — Canopius 4444 or Beazley 623 are ideal (existing tech equipment books)
2. **Find a Lloyd's broker** — acts as intermediary between Corgi and the syndicate
3. **Build the policy management system** — Corgi's existing platform could serve as the basis
4. **Apply through ATLAS** — with broker and managing agent sponsorship
5. **Pass the live system demo** — demonstrate underwriting, pricing, and administration capabilities
6. **Get approved** — fast-track is now 2 weeks; standard is a few months

### Alternative: Syndicate-in-a-Box (SiaB)
If Corgi wanted to go further than coverholder and have its own syndicate:
- **Application fee:** £100,000 (non-refundable)
- Can write **<£100m GWP in year 1** (fine for initial GPU RVG volumes)
- Must have projected **net operating expense ratio <35% by year 3**
- Must demonstrate **innovation and differentiation** (GPU RVG qualifies)
- Initial application: **5 PowerPoint slides** covering team, opportunity, and why Lloyd's
- Need to partner with a **managing agent** by Business Opportunities Committee stage
- **4-year timeline** to get internal model approval (vs 3 years for traditional syndicate)
- Performance reviewed in year 3 against agreed KPIs

## 5. Lloyd's Classes of Business — Where GPU RVG Fits

Lloyd's uses risk codes to classify business. GPU RVG could fit under several:

| Risk Code Area | Relevance to GPU RVG |
|----------------|---------------------|
| **Property — Equipment/Machinery** | Physical asset protection, all-risks coverage for GPU hardware |
| **Engineering — Electronic Equipment Insurance (EEI)** | Standard Lloyd's class for electronic/computer equipment |
| **Engineering — Machinery Breakdown (MB)** | Equipment failure and performance degradation |
| **Technology E&O** | Performance guarantees on technology products |
| **Innovation ICX** | Catch-all for innovative products — up to 5% additional GWP |
| **Specialty** | Novel risk classes that don't fit traditional categories |

### The Innovation ICX Route
- Any syndicate can write GPU RVG under the **Innovation ICX class** using up to 5% additional GWP
- Performance is **not counted against BAU oversight** — syndicate faces no downside risk to their metrics
- Lloyd's explicitly permits "small experiments" to establish data for new risk classes
- **This is the lowest-friction way to get GPU RVG into the Lloyd's market**
