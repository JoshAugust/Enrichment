## 1. TACC — Texas Advanced Computing Center

| Field | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **University** | University of Texas at Austin |
| **Location** | Austin, TX |
| **Website** | tacc.utexas.edu |

### GPU Systems Available
| System | GPUs | Notes |
|--------|------|-------|
| **Vista** (2024) | NVIDIA H100 (Grace-Hopper architecture) | First AI-focused TACC resource; part of NAIRR pilot |
| **Stampede3** | Intel Max Series GPUs | Only NSF ACCESS system with Intel Max GPUs; also flagship HPC |
| **Frontera** | NVIDIA RTX GPUs | NSF Leadership-class; predecessor flagship |

### Commercial Access Programs
- **STAR Industry Program** — Primary commercial channel. Annual membership (minimum 1-year commitment). Provides supercomputing time, technical support, visualization resources, 1:1 training, workshops.

  - Reduced rates available for small firms and startups
- **Frontera Allocations for Commercial Orgs** — U.S. commercial organizations (esp. small businesses) may apply for allocations; TACC especially welcomes cooperative projects pairing industry and universities

### Pricing
- Not publicly listed. Contact TACC directly via the STAR program page.
- Small firm discounts available on inquiry.

## 4. NCSA — National Center for Supercomputing Applications

| Field | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **University** | University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign (UIUC) |
| **Location** | Urbana, IL |
| **Website** | ncsa.illinois.edu |

### GPU Systems Available
| System | GPUs | Notes |
|--------|------|-------|
| **Delta** | 400× NVIDIA A100 (40GB), 400× A40 (48GB), 40× A100 dense config, 8× H200 (141GB HBM) | Largest x86 GPU resource in NSF portfolio; H200 nodes = largest memory GPU in NSF |
| **DeltaAI** | (Next-gen; entered production 2024) | Second NSF supercomputer at NCSA in 3 years |
| **Radiant** | Additional GPU nodes (A100s added 2024) | Illinois Computes initiative |

### Commercial Access Programs
- **Industry Partner Program (IPP)** — 35+ years old; executes dozens of projects annually; >$10M annual revenue. Includes access to NCSA expertise, infrastructure, and UIUC faculty/students.
- **vForge** — Dedicated commercial HPC platform for industry. Charged per node-hour via Slurm. Includes GPU nodes, standard compute, 50TB Taiga storage included (additional at $50.77/TB/year).
- **CDA Industry Partner Program** — New program created July 2024 expanding commercial engagement
- Cost-recovery basis for proprietary usage on Delta

### Pricing
- vForge: Node-hour based billing (specific rates not public). Storage: **$50.77/TB/year**.
- GPU node rates: contact help@ncsa.illinois.edu or (217) 244-0710

## 6. MIT Lincoln Laboratory

| Field | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **University** | MIT (FFRDC — Federally Funded Research and Development Center) |
| **Location** | Lexington, MA |
| **Website** | ll.mit.edu |

### GPU Systems Available
| System | GPUs | Notes |
|--------|------|-------|
| **TX-GAIA** (2019) | 896× NVIDIA V100 Tensor Core GPUs | 448 HPE Apollo 2000 nodes; 100 AI petaflops; was #1 university AI supercomputer at launch |

### Commercial Access Programs
- **No direct commercial GPU time sales.** As an FFRDC, MIT LL is prohibited from competing with the private sector.
- Limited engagement pathways:
  - **CRADAs** (Cooperative R&D Agreements) — Joint R&D with private companies
  - **Test Agreements** — Companies can have technology tested at LL facilities
  - **Technology Transfer** — Licensing of government-sponsored IP
- TX-GAIA access: open to MIT community members and federal sponsors only

### Pricing
- N/A for commercial GPU time
- CRADA/Test agreements are negotiated case-by-case with Technology Transfer Office

### Key Leadership
- LLSC (Lincoln Laboratory Supercomputing Center) management team not publicly named on leadership pages
- LL Director: **Eric Evans** (Laboratory Director)

> **Assessment:** Not a GPU compute sales target. FFRDC legal restrictions block commercial competition. Engagement only viable via CRADA/joint research.

### B. Penguin Computing / Penguin Solutions (POD)

| Field | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **Origin** | Commercial (NOT a university spinout) |
| **Founded** | 1998, San Francisco, CA |

**Notable Academic Ties:** Operates GPU clusters for universities (e.g., NYU: 160 AMD GPUs, 20 dedicated). Strong academic partnership model — sells/operates HPC infrastructure for universities.

**Pricing:** 
- Historical benchmark: ~$0.101/core-hour (older reference; GPU pricing separate)
- Current GPU rates: not publicly listed; quote-based via pod.penguincomputing.com

**Assessment:** Strong HPC credibility and existing university relationships. More of a HPC infrastructure vendor/cloud than a commercial access reseller of academic compute.

## Key Findings & Notes

1. **Best commercial access targets:** TACC (STAR), NCSA (vForge/IPP), and SDSC (Expanse Industry) are the clearest commercial pathways among true academic HPC centers.

2. **NCSA is most mature commercially** — 35+ year industry program, $10M+ annual revenue, dedicated vForge platform. Most "enterprise-ready" of the academic centers.

3. **SDSC's Plexus portal** is notable — Core Scientific partnership makes it more self-service than typical academic HPC billing, which is unusual.

4. **NCAR and MIT LL are effectively closed** to commercial GPU compute sales. NCAR is NSF-mission locked; MIT LL is FFRDC-constrained.

5. **Stanford HAI and BAIR** are research collaboration bodies, not compute providers. Engagement there is through research sponsorship, not GPU time purchase.

6. **Cirrascale and Penguin** are not university spinouts — both are purely commercial HPC companies with strong academic *customer* relationships, not academic origins.

7. **Pricing opacity is universal** — none of the academic centers publish GPU time rates publicly. All require direct inquiry. Expect cost-recovery models (cheaper than AWS/Azure but with academic procurement friction).

8. **NCAR restructuring risk (2026):** NSF is actively restructuring NCAR; CU Boulder has proposed a takeover. Monitor for changes that could alter access policies.
