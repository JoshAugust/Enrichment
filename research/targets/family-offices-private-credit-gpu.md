# Family Offices & Private Credit Funds — GPU Infrastructure Lending

**Research Date:** March 2026  
**Purpose:** Target mapping for GPU-backed lending / AI infrastructure finance outreach  
**Scope:** BDCs, private credit funds, alternative asset managers, equipment finance firms, and family office-adjacent vehicles active in GPU-collateralized or AI infrastructure debt

---

## Market Context

- GPU-backed lending has unlocked **$11B+** for AI "neocloud" startups at advance rates of 50–70%, interest rates 12–15%
- Morgan Stanley projects **$1.5T funding shortfall** for data centers through 2028, with private credit expected to fill ~$800B
- Structure: GPUs + customer off-take contracts as collateral; typical loan-to-value 50–70%; durations 3–5 years
- Key borrowers: CoreWeave, Lambda, Fluidstack, Crusoe, nscale, 5C Group, xAI

## Key Observations for Outreach

1. **Two clear clusters:** (a) large alt managers (Blackstone, Blue Owl, Apollo, PIMCO) doing $1B+ structured GPU debt; (b) specialized/smaller players (DigitalBridge Credit, Upper90, Trinity, Magnetar) who pioneered the trade
2. **Magnetar and DigitalBridge Credit** are the highest-conviction targets — they wrote the playbook and will likely want to back the next wave
3. **Trinity Capital** is the most accessible BDC for equipment finance approaches — public company, mid-market focus, GPU lending already in mandate
4. **Valor Compute Infrastructure (VCI)** is the template for a dedicated GPU infrastructure fund — useful both as a comp and potential LP/co-investor
5. **Family office angle:** most pure family offices are LPs in the above vehicles rather than direct lenders; the "family office" exposure to GPU debt is primarily through feeder structures into Magnetar, Coatue, Brookfield, and DigitalBridge
6. **Advance rates:** 50–70% LTV on NVIDIA H100/H200/Blackwell; interest 12–15%; most structures require take-or-pay customer contracts as secondary collateral
7. **Risk:** Technological obsolescence is the bear case — all lenders underwrite to current depreciation curves; Blackwell replacing Hopper may stress older loan books
