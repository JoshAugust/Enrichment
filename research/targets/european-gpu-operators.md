## Key Insights for Insurance Product Targeting

### Ideal Customer Profile
1. **Revenue:** $50M–$500M
2. **GPU fleet value:** $20M–$300M
3. **Financing:** Debt-financed GPU acquisition (term loans, asset-backed facilities)
4. **Ownership:** Owns (not rents) GPU hardware
5. **Growth stage:** Series A–C, or recently public, needing to protect asset value for lenders

### Top 5 Priority Targets (Best Insurance Fit)
1. **Domyn** (Italy) — €325M+ debt-financed GPU build; seeking billions more
2. **NexGen Cloud** (UK) — Mid-market, $1B capex plan on thin equity
3. **Gcore** (Luxembourg) — First external funding, expanding GPU fleet rapidly
4. **Verda** (Finland) — Classic VC-funded GPU operator, $85M raised
5. **Genesis Cloud** (Germany) — Seed-stage but hardware-owning; perfect entry point

### Key Financing Trend
GPU-backed debt financing is becoming standard in Europe. **Nscale's $1.4B term loan** and **FluidStack's $10B Macquarie facility** are both explicitly secured by GPU assets — creating direct demand for residual value insurance to protect lender interests.

### Regulatory Tailwinds
- EU data sovereignty requirements force European GPU build-out (can't just rent from US hyperscalers)
- EuroHPC AI Factories programme creating dozens of new GPU-owning entities
- France's €109B AI commitment and Germany's sovereign AI push accelerating deployment
