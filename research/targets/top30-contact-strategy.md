Before diving into contacts, here's the toolchain for verifying any email address constructed from known patterns:

| Tool | Use Case | Cost | Notes |

| **Hunter.io** | Find + verify emails by domain; domain search reveals company-wide patterns | Free: 25/mo; $34/mo for 500 | Highest accuracy (~71%); shows confirmed pattern per domain |
| **Apollo.io** | Prospecting database (275M+ contacts); find emails + titles | Free tier: unlimited email credits | Best for finding people by title at a company; shows verified vs unverified |
| **ZeroBounce** | SMTP-level verification; catch-all detection | $99/mo for 25K verifications | Best deliverability guarantee (<2% bounce); use to verify constructed emails |
| **LinkedIn Sales Navigator** | Confirm current title + company; InMail as backup channel | $99/mo | Essential for confirming someone is still in role before emailing |
| **Google Dorking** | `site:businesswire.com "firstname.lastname@domain.com"` | Free | Press releases often contain direct emails; most reliable public source |
| **SEC EDGAR** | 10-K/proxy filings list officers with contact details | Free | For public companies (CRWV, APLD, IREN, FIHL, etc.) |

### Verification Workflow (Per Contact)
1. **Confirm the person is still in role** → LinkedIn profile check
2. **Identify the email pattern** → Hunter.io domain search or confirmed press release
3. **Construct the email** → Apply pattern to the person's name
4. **Verify deliverability** → ZeroBounce single-email verify (SMTP check)
5. **Fallback** → Apollo.io lookup; if still unverified, use LinkedIn InMail or company contact form
