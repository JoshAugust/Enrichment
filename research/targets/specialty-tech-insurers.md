# Specialty Technology Asset Insurers & Reinsurers

> Research compiled: 2026-03-29

## 🔬 Tier 1 — Pure-Play / Deep Specialist (Most Targeted)

### 1. Advanced Technology Assurance (ATA)
- **Type:** London-based MGU (Managing General Underwriter)
- **Specialty:** AI infrastructure & data center multi-line coverage
- **Differentiator:** Launched first-of-kind $750M aggregated global insurance facility (Oct 2023) for AI and data center buildout under a **single multi-line policy** — combining property damage, cyber, marine cargo, EIL, war & terrorism
- **Backers/Capacity:** Consortium of 10+ global (re)insurers including Lloyd's syndicates, Arch Insurance International, Munich Re Specialty, SCOR, Convex Group (cornerstone)
- **Website:** (private/limited public presence; part of London market)
- **Why Interesting:** Created specifically for the AI infrastructure wave; the policy structure alone is novel

---

### 2. Parametrix Insurance
- **Type:** Insurtech / Parametric MGA
- **Specialty:** Parametric cloud outage & system failure business interruption for tech businesses
- **Differentiator:** Triggers based on measurable downtime thresholds (not loss assessment); covers AWS, Azure, GCP outages; also offers SLA breach coverage for data centers; standalone system-failure parametric BI product
- **Products:** Traditional cyber with parametric BI trigger; standalone parametric BI for cloud/system failure
- **Website:** parametrixinsurance.com
- **Why Interesting:** Only parametric insurer specifically focused on cloud/tech infrastructure downtime

---

### 3. Descartes Underwriting
- **Type:** Global MGA / Parametric specialist (Paris-founded, 2019)
- **Specialty:** Parametric insurance for data centers — catastrophe, construction delay, BI, NDBI, SLA breach
- **Differentiator:** Up to $140M parametric capacity per policy against up to 20 natural perils (tornado, flood, hurricane, EQ, extreme heat, deep freeze); covers construction phase (DSU) through operations
- **Products:** Data center natural peril parametric suite; deductible buy-down; project finance protection
- **Website:** descartesunderwriting.com
- **Why Interesting:** Climate-science-driven parametric specifically architected for data center lifecycle risks

---

### 4. FM Global / FM Intellium
- **Type:** Mutual insurer with dedicated data center unit
- **Specialty:** Property & equipment insurance for data centers and AI/cloud infrastructure
- **Differentiator:** 25+ years data center experience; insures 1,100+ data centers (~$250B value); up to $5B per risk capacity; FM Intellium (launched May 2025) is a dedicated program for data center/AI buildout with on-demand SME unit
- **Website:** fm.com/solutions/services-we-provide/intellium
- **Why Interesting:** The dominant large-limit property insurer for mission-critical tech infrastructure; unique engineering-led approach (30,000 hrs/year in data centers)

---

### 5. HSB (Hartford Steam Boiler) — A Munich Re Company
- **Type:** Specialty insurer / Munich Re subsidiary
- **Specialty:** Equipment breakdown insurance, including technology hardware and data systems
- **Differentiator:** Deep equipment breakdown expertise; TechAdvantage™ product for commercial equipment; separate computer/technology insurance line covering hardware, data loss, virus/hacking damage, increased costs
- **Products:** EDP (Electronic Data Processing) equipment; commercial equipment breakdown; computer insurance (UK/Ireland)
- **Website:** munichre.com/hsb
- **Why Interesting:** One of the oldest equipment breakdown specialists in the world; often used as a carrier behind programs

## Key Themes & Observations

1. **The ATA Facility is a signal**: The $750M multi-line AI/data center facility launched by ATA (with Convex, SCOR, Arch, Munich Re, Lloyd's syndicates) shows the market is actively organizing around AI infrastructure as a standalone risk class.

2. **Parametric is the frontier**: Both Parametrix (cloud downtime) and Descartes (physical nat-cat) are building parametric products specifically for tech infrastructure — a space traditional carriers can't easily enter.

3. **Lloyd's market is the deepest well**: For non-standard, complex, or large-limit tech asset risks, the Lloyd's market (Canopius, TMK, ATA facility, SCOR 2015) remains the primary home.

4. **Equipment breakdown is underrated**: HSB/Munich Re and the EDP insurance route (via inland marine and commercial property) is a well-established but less-discussed path — Berkley Fire & Marine, RLI, and Sompo all use it.

5. **Insurtechs are filling the prototype/startup gap**: Vouch and Superscript are specifically capturing risks (startup hardware, prototypes, demo equipment) that legacy carriers decline or under-price.
