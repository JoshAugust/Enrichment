## Context: The GPU/RVI Opportunity

Residual Value Insurance (RVI) at Lloyd's has historically been written for aircraft, marine vessels, vehicles, and commercial equipment. There is now an emerging opportunity around **GPU/AI hardware RVI**:

- GPU ABS issuance projected to grow from ~$8B (2025) to ~$25B by 2028
- H100 GPUs lose 40–60% value within 18–24 months of successor launch
- Lightly used H100s (1–2 yr) retain ~70–85% of new value; moderate use (2–3 yr) ~50–70%
- Lambda Labs securitized $500M in GPU-backed debt in late 2025 (first compute ABS)
- Ornn Compute Exchange launched GPU Residual Value Swaps (RVS) in 2025
- Lloyd's broker **Iris Insurance Brokers** launched a dedicated RVI Centre of Excellence in 2025
- Lloyd's broker **MNK Re** also has a dedicated RVI CoE (established ~2019, expanded)

The Lloyd's market does not yet have an established GPU RVI product. The opportunity is to build one — likely as a facility backed by one or more syndicates below.

## Structural Notes for GPU RVI at Lloyd's

### Why Lloyd's is the Right Market
- Lloyd's accommodates non-standard/emerging risk classes without requiring a admitted market filing
- Syndicate capital structure allows for phased capacity build
- Multiple syndicates can co-participate on a facility (follow market structure)
- Lloyd's Lab (innovation accelerator) could be an entry point for novel tech asset coverage

### Product Structure Options
1. **Pure Residual Value Insurance** — Indemnifies lender/lessor if GPU sale proceeds at lease end < insured residual value. Requires depreciation model / appraisal methodology.
2. **Asset Protection (Physical Damage)** — Broader property cover for GPU hardware clusters including breakdown, transit, all-risk.
3. **Collateral Protection Insurance (CPI)** — Protects lender collateral (GPU as loan security) against value impairment.
4. **Equipment Breakdown** — Mechanical/electrical breakdown of GPU/compute hardware (often written as an endorsement on property policies).
5. **Marine Cargo + Storage** — Covers GPU in transit and at warehouse before deployment.

### Key Underwriting Challenges
- **Technological obsolescence** — GPU value depreciates faster than traditional equipment; requires custom depreciation curves
- **Secondary market liquidity** — GPU secondary market exists but is nascent; pricing data limited
- **Concentration risk** — Large GPU clusters = large per-risk accumulation
- **Counterparty credit** — Lessee default risk (not typically covered under RVI — RVI is pure asset value risk)

### Lead Syndicate Recommendation
Best candidates to **lead** a GPU RVI facility (i.e., set terms, take largest line):
1. **Beazley 2623** — Broadest tech specialty capability + appetite for innovation
2. **Ascot 1414** — Explicit tech hardware E&O + specie/cargo infrastructure
3. **Canopius 4444** — Dedicated tech industry product + largest capacity vehicle

Best candidates as **follow markets**:
- Munich Re 457 (cargo/specie expertise)
- Brit 2987 (cyber-physical overlap)
- Hiscox 33 (strong property book)
