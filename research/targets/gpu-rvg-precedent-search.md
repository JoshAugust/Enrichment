## KEY FINDING: GPU Residual Value Insurance ALREADY EXISTS — But Not as a Standalone Product

Agent research confirmed that **specialist insurers are already providing GPU residual value guarantees** as part of GPU ABS deals, but their identities are not publicly disclosed. This is the most important finding of the precedent search.

### What We Know

1. **GPU ABS deals routinely include residual value insurance/guarantees** to bolster credit quality — mirroring auto lease ABS structures.

2. **The standard structure:** An insurer guarantees that at least **40% of the GPUs' original value** will be realized at disposition. If market value plunges >60%, insurance reimburses the trust up to the 40% floor, capping loss severity.

3. **First GPU ABS deal** (Lambda Labs, 2025, $1.1B AAA-rated via Macquarie) used this exact 40% residual value floor structure.

4. **Who provides it:** Sources reference "specialist insurers" and potentially "monoline insurers" — specific provider names are NOT publicly disclosed. This is Corgi's opportunity: the market exists but there is no branded, standalone GPU RVG product.

5. **NVIDIA buy-back guarantees** are mentioned as an alternative to insurance wraps — some deals may use NVIDIA OEM guarantees instead of third-party insurance.

6. **"Some specialist insurers now offer obsolescence insurance"** — paying out if resale values fall below threshold due to new tech release. This is functionally identical to what Corgi is building.

7. **For rating agency credit substitution,** the insurer/guarantor must be highly rated OR collateralize its obligation.

### Structural Parallels

- Auto lease ABS residual value insurance (most direct parallel)
- Aircraft residual value insurance (mature market, well-understood actuarial methodology)
- Equipment lease residual value guarantees

### Implications for Corgi

1. **The market validation is stronger than expected.** GPU RVG isn't theoretical — it's already being done on a bespoke, deal-by-deal basis inside ABS structures. The opportunity is to productize and brand it.

2. **The competitive landscape is opaque.** Unknown specialist insurers are providing these guarantees. Identifying them is critical — they could be competitors, partners, or capacity providers.

3. **Rating agencies already have methodology** for evaluating GPU residual value guarantees (used in Lambda ABS rating). This means Corgi doesn't need to educate rating agencies from scratch.

4. **The 40% floor is the market standard** — Corgi should price around this benchmark initially.

5. **Monoline insurer involvement** suggests the capital markets insurance community (not just P&C) is relevant. Firms like Assured Guaranty, MBIA/National, or specialist equipment wrap providers may be the unnamed players.
