## Sub-group Observations

### 🔴 Tier S — 90–100 Points: Act Now
**CoreWeave, nscale, Crusoe Energy** — All three have confirmed GPU-backed debt structures with named lenders. Their lenders (Blackstone, Blue Owl, PIMCO, Brookfield) almost certainly require insurance as a condition of lending. These are the clearest near-term targets for specialty GPU equipment insurance.

### 🟠 Tier 1 — 80–89 Points: High Priority
**HUMAIN, G42/Core42, Lambda Labs, Stargate JV, Deutsche Telekom, Orange, Yotta, Cassava, Nebius, xAI/Valor, Oracle** — Mix of mega-scale sovereign AI operators and confirmed debt-financed GPU clouds. All have large replacement-cost exposure and operational dependence on GPU hardware.

### 🟡 Tier 2 — 70–79 Points: Strong Pipeline
**SK Telecom, SoftBank, Aramco Digital, Firmus, Fluidstack** + a cluster of mid-tier GPU cloud companies (Applied Digital, Bit Digital, IREN, Singtel, NTT, NEOM, Together AI, Vultr, Voltage Park, Qai). Many have confirmed debt or likely equipment financing. Strong contact coverage.

### 🟢 Tier 3 — 60–69 Points: Qualified Prospects
Includes regional players (FPT Vietnam, Kasi Cloud, iXAfrica), energy-sector GPU operators (ExxonMobil), and hyperscalers (Microsoft, Amazon, Google) who likely self-insure but may need specific GPU fleet coverage at scale.

### ⚪ Tier 4 — 50–59 Points: Longer-term / Watch
Government labs (ORNL, LLNL, LANL) have massive fleets but no debt financing. Telcos (Verizon, KT Corp), smaller public GPU companies (SMCI, Recursion), and energy majors (Chevron, TotalEnergies) are possible but lower urgency.

## Methodology Notes

1. **Debt-financing is the swing factor** — companies with confirmed GPU-backed debt structures (CoreWeave, nscale, Crusoe, Lambda, Fluidstack, 5C Group, xAI/Valor) are the highest-priority targets because their lenders contractually require collateral insurance.
2. **Sovereign AI operators score well on 'b' but lower on 'c'** — HUMAIN, G42, SDAIA, TII often use government/sovereign financing rather than commercial debt, reducing lender-mandated insurance pressure.
3. **Government labs (ORNL, LLNL, LANL) have massive fleets but zero debt** — useful for benchmarking but not commercial insurance targets.
4. **Hyperscalers (Microsoft, Amazon, Google) are wildcards** — their fleet values are enormous (25/25) but they likely already have sophisticated captive structures and the relevant decision-makers are buried deep in Risk Management.
5. **Reachability bonus** — companies with confirmed emails (Crusoe, Lambda, Together AI, nscale, IREN, Voltage Park, Cassava) are operationally prioritized even if their scores are similar to companies with only named executives.
