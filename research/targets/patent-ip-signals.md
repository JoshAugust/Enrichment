## 1. GPU Cooling, Rack Design & Power Management Patents

### NVIDIA Corporation
- **Total patent portfolio:** ~6,963 applications at USPTO, 4,810 granted (88.44% grant rate)
- **Key GPU infrastructure patents:**
  - **Intelligent movable racks** — patents covering integrated data center racks with computing equipment, power systems, cooling systems, and cabling managed by a unified controller
  - **Improved data center cooling systems** — heat exchange devices managing multiple cooling loops for different computing system components
  - **Resource scheduling & profiling** — "Scheduling and Resource Management Based on Application Profiling" — AI-based prediction of CPU/GPU resource needs per user/application
  - **Hybrid distributed compute** — resource allocation patents for segmenting tasks across CPU, GPU, and QPU using ML models
  - **Task scheduling** (130 filings in 2024 alone)
- **Signal:** NVIDIA is patenting the full stack — chip, rack, cooling, scheduling, orchestration. Dominant IP moat.

### Vertiv Holdings
- **Key move:** Acquired **CoolTera Ltd.** (Dec 2023) — described as "a strategic acquisition of critical patents and intellectual property" for liquid cooling CDU infrastructure
- **Technology patents:** Pumped two-phase (P2P) direct-to-chip cooling — validated at ASME InterPACK 2024
- **Partnership IP:** Joint development with GRC for immersion cooling
- **Signal:** Actively acquiring cooling IP to own the liquid cooling technology stack. Major infrastructure play.

### Schneider Electric
- **Key move:** Acquired **Motivair** (Oct 2024) for **$850M** — added cold plate technology and CDU manufacturing patents
- **Immersion cooling:** Partnership with **Iceotope** — claims first commercially available integrated rack with chassis-based immersive liquid cooling
- **NVIDIA collaboration:** Co-developed first publicly available AI data center reference designs
- **Signal:** Buying its way into cooling IP leadership. Motivair acquisition was the largest cooling-focused M&A of 2024.

### Asetek A/S
- **Patent portfolio:** 152 patents globally (49 patent families), 92 currently active
- **Key patents:**
  - Thermal interposer liquid cooling for GPUs (USPTO allowed)
  - RackCDU™ ISAC™ (In-Server Air Conditioning) technology
  - RackCDU D2C™ (Direct-to-Chip) — captures 60-80% of server heat
  - **Foundational AIO liquid cooler patent (US 8240362B2) expired May 2025** — significant: opens up competitive landscape
- **GPU-specific:** Patent allowed on GPU thermal interposer liquid cooling
- **Signal:** Patent pioneer, but key patent expiry creates opportunity for competitors. Still active in data center direct-to-chip.

### Green Revolution Cooling (GRC)
- **Patents:** 11 granted patents for single-phase immersion cooling, 10 more pending
- **Differentiation:** 14+ years operating longest-running immersion-cooled data center
- **Partnership:** Business Collaboration Agreement with Samsung C&T for immersion-cooled reference designs
- **Signal:** Deep immersion cooling IP. Niche but defensible patent position.

### CoolIT Systems
- **Patent litigation:** Won patent lawsuit vs. Asetek (Sept 2022) — court ruled CoolIT products don't infringe Asetek's patents
- **Active in:** Direct liquid cooling for GPU servers (Gigabyte partnership)
- **Signal:** Proven ability to design around competitor patents. Active but less publicly disclosed portfolio.

### Qualcomm
- **US20170061568A1:** "Characterizing GPU workloads and power management using command stream hinting"
- **Signal:** Cross-domain IP from mobile GPU power management applicable to data center workloads.

### State Grid Corporation of China
- **Major patent holder** in AI-powered data center operations — portfolio covers power management and dynamic resource scheduling
- Key filings: CN113282368A (edge computing scheduling), CN114281544A (AI-driven power task execution), CN111813502B (computing resource management)
- **Signal:** Chinese state infrastructure IP — relevant for anyone tracking geopolitical GPU infrastructure dynamics.

## 3. GPU Depreciation, Residual Value & Hardware Lifecycle Patents

### Findings
- **No patents found** specifically covering GPU depreciation models, residual value prediction, or hardware lifecycle management using ML/AI
- This appears to be a **genuine white space** in the patent landscape
- Companies managing GPU fleets (CoreWeave, Lambda, hyperscalers) likely handle depreciation internally but have not filed IP around predictive models

### Adjacent IP Areas
- IT asset lifecycle management patents exist (IBM, ServiceNow) but are not GPU-specific
- Financial modeling patents exist for equipment residual value but predate the GPU infrastructure era
- **Opportunity signal:** A company filing patents in GPU-specific depreciation/residual value prediction would be signaling deep infrastructure sophistication and long-term thinking

### Companies to Watch
- **Core Scientific** — closest with data center infrastructure monitoring patents that could extend to lifecycle analytics
- **NVIDIA** — resource profiling patents could extend to hardware lifecycle prediction
- **Any neocloud filing here** would be a strong signal of maturity (none found yet)

## 5. Key Takeaways & Investment Signals

### 🔴 Strongest Patent Moats (Most Defensible)
1. **NVIDIA** — patenting every infrastructure layer (chip → rack → cooling → scheduling → orchestration)
2. **Groq** — 63-103 patents around inference architecture; $20B licensing deal validates IP
3. **Cerebras** — 102 patents on wafer-scale architecture; partnerships with OpenAI, Amazon
4. **Vertiv** — aggressively acquiring cooling IP (CoolTera acquisition)
5. **Schneider Electric** — $850M Motivair buy for cooling patent stack

### 🟡 Operational Moats Without Patent Protection
- CoreWeave, Lambda, RunPod, Vast.ai, Together AI — competing on speed, relationships, and capital, not IP
- Vulnerable to commoditization as cooling/rack technology standardizes

### 🟢 White Space Opportunities
- **GPU depreciation & residual value prediction** — no patents found; first mover could own the category
- **GPU-specific lifecycle management** — adjacent to existing IT asset management but unpatented for GPU infrastructure
- **Cooling IP post-Asetek expiry** — foundational liquid cooling patent expired May 2025, opening competitive landscape
