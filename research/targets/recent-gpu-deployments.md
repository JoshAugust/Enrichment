## Key Observations for Insurance Product Design

1. **GPU-as-Collateral is now mainstream** — Lambda's Macquarie deal explicitly uses GPUs as loan collateral. CoreWeave's entire debt stack is implicitly GPU-backed. This creates direct demand for residual value insurance from lenders.

2. **Project finance structures dominate** — Crusoe's $9.6B JPMorgan-led debt for a single data center project is infrastructure-scale project finance. These structures traditionally require asset insurance.

3. **Depreciation risk is real** — GPU generations turn over every 12-18 months. H100 → H200 → Blackwell → Vera Rubin. Residual value on 2-3 year old GPUs is a genuine risk for 5-year debt structures.

4. **Concentration risk** — xAI has 230,000+ GPUs at a single Memphis site. Physical catastrophe exposure is enormous.

5. **Sovereign buyers need different products** — Government-backed projects have credit strength but unique political/regulatory risks. Export controls (especially US→Middle East) add a policy risk layer.

6. **AMD entry changes dynamics** — OpenAI and Meta both making massive AMD commitments. Residual value for AMD AI GPUs has no track record, making insurance more valuable (and harder to price).

7. **The lender ecosystem is the buyer** — JPMorgan, Macquarie, Blackstone, Blue Owl, Brookfield are all extending GPU-backed credit. They are the natural insurance buyers.
