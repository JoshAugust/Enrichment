## PART 2: Free Tool Contact Discovery Playbook {#part-2}

> **Credit conservation principle:** Treat every free credit like it costs $5. Always research the target on LinkedIn first, confirm they're the right person, then spend the credit. Never spray.

---

### TOOL 1: Hunter.io
**Free tier:** 25 searches + 25 verifications per month (as of 2026)  
**Best for:** Finding email format patterns for a domain; verifying emails in bulk

#### Setup
1. Go to [hunter.io](https://hunter.io) → click **Sign up free**
2. Sign up with a work email (not Gmail — Hunter flags personal addresses for lower accuracy)
3. No credit card required
4. Dashboard unlocks immediately

#### How to Use Free Credits Wisely

**Domain Search (use for pattern discovery, not individual lookups):**
1. Go to **Domain Search** tab
2. Enter company domain (e.g., `coreweave.com`)
3. Hunter shows you: email format (firstname.lastname@, flastname@, etc.), confidence score, all publicly found emails
4. **This is 1 credit** — but you learn the format for ALL staff at that company
5. Write the format down → use it to guess any contact's email without spending more credits

**Email Finder (use only to confirm a specific high-value contact):**
1. Go to **Email Finder**
2. Enter first name, last name, and domain
3. Returns the email + confidence score
4. **This is 1 credit** — only use when you already know the format is unclear

**Email Verifier:**
1. Paste an email address → Hunter tells you if it exists (deliverable, risky, undeliverable)
2. Use this on emails you've guessed from the pattern before cold-emailing

#### Maximize Free Credits Strategy
- **Never use Email Finder if you already know the pattern** — just construct it manually
- Use 1 Domain Search credit per company → derive all contacts from the pattern
- Save all 25 searches for: 5 GPU operators, 5 lenders, 5 reinsurers, 5 cross-category, 5 high-value wildcards
- Use verification credits only on your top 25 manually-constructed emails

### TOOL 2: Apollo.io
**Free tier:** 10 credits/month for exports; unlimited prospect search; email reveals limited  
**Best for:** Building lead lists, searching by title/company/tech stack, enriching contacts

#### Setup
1. Go to [apollo.io](https://apollo.io) → **Get started free**
2. Sign up with work email
3. Install the **Apollo Chrome Extension** — this is essential for LinkedIn enrichment
4. No credit card required

#### How to Use Free Tier

**People Search (unlimited browsing, limited reveals):**
1. Click **Search** → **People**
2. Set filters:
   - Job Title: paste from Part 1 searches above
   - Location: United States, United Kingdom, Bermuda
   - Industry: Computer Software / Financial Services / Insurance
   - Employee count: 11–500
3. Browse results → identify your top contacts
4. Click **Access email** → this costs 1 credit
5. You get email + sometimes direct phone

**LinkedIn Extension (most powerful free use):**
1. Install Chrome extension
2. Open anyone's LinkedIn profile
3. Apollo sidebar appears → shows email if it's in their DB **without costing credits** in some cases
4. If email shows as "Request" → that costs 1 credit

**Company Enrichment:**
1. Go to **Accounts** tab → search for a company
2. See all employees in Apollo's DB, technology stack, funding data
3. Filter by title to find the right person without spending credits

#### Maximize Credits
- Browse extensively, reveal sparingly — **only spend credits on people you intend to email within 48 hours**
- Use Apollo's **Sequences** (free tier allows 1 sequence) to track outreach
- Export your saved people list as CSV (limited on free tier) to cross-reference with other tools

#### Priority Targets for Apollo Credits
Spend reveals on people whose emails couldn't be derived from Hunter's pattern + whose role is C-Suite or VP level in your ICP.

### TOOL 3: Skrapp.io
**Free tier:** 150 credits/month  
**Best for:** LinkedIn → email extraction (better LinkedIn integration than most tools)

#### Setup
1. Go to [skrapp.io](https://skrapp.io) → **Sign up free**
2. Install the **Skrapp Chrome Extension**
3. Sign in via the extension
4. Free account = 150 email find credits/month

#### How to Use
1. Open any LinkedIn profile
2. Skrapp extension button appears in the browser bar
3. Click **Find Email** → Skrapp searches its DB and web for the email
4. Returns email + confidence score
5. **Each successful find = 1 credit** (failed searches don't charge)

**Bulk LinkedIn Search Mode:**
1. Run a LinkedIn search (even without Sales Navigator)
2. Go through profiles page by page
3. Skrapp can do **bulk reveals** from a LinkedIn search results page
4. Be careful — this can consume credits quickly; only use on pre-filtered searches

#### Maximize Credits
- 150 credits is generous — use it for ALL your GPU operator targets first (highest ROI)
- Skrapp is better than most tools at finding emails for non-US targets (good for Bermuda, UK)
- Cross-reference: if Skrapp returns a low confidence score, verify with Hunter before emailing

## PART 3: Phone Number Discovery Strategy {#part-3}

> **Principle:** Direct dials close deals. IVR navigation is a skill. Document everything you learn.

---

### IVR Navigation Framework

When you call a company main line, use this sequence:

1. **Ask for the person by full name** — "Can you put me through to [First Last]?"
2. If they ask your name: use your name + company + one-line reason ("calling about infrastructure financing options")
3. If they say "they're not available": **ask for their direct line or extension**
4. If they say "I'll take a message": ask "Is there a direct email or direct line I can use to reach them?" — gatekeepers often give this without thinking
5. If you reach voicemail: leave a 20-second message — name, company, one sentence of value, callback number. Then hang up. Do not pitch.

---

### Direct Dial Discovery Techniques

**Technique 1: Conference Archive Search**
- Search Google: `"[Name]" "[Company]" site:linkedin.com OR site:legalrecruitment.com OR site:crunchbase.com`
- Many conference bios list direct contact info
- Search: `"[Name]" "[Company]" "direct" OR "mobile" OR "dial"`

**Technique 2: SEC/EDGAR/Companies House Filings**
- Public companies file contact details in 10-K, proxy statements, and annual reports
- For UK companies: search [Companies House](https://find-and-update.company-information.service.gov.uk/)
- For Bermuda: [Bermuda Monetary Authority](https://www.bma.bm/) registrations sometimes include contact info

**Technique 3: Event/Webinar Registration Pages**
- Past conference speaker pages often include bios with contact email
- Search: `"[Name]" "[Company]" "speaker" OR "panelist" site:[conferencewebsite].com`
- Key conferences: SuperReturn, IMN, ELFA, RIMS, BILTIR (Bermuda ILS)

**Technique 4: Press Release Mining**
- Search: `"[Company]" "contact" "press" OR "media" site:prnewswire.com OR site:businesswire.com`
- PR contacts are often connected to senior leadership; they'll forward or provide direct lines

**Technique 5: LinkedIn Message First, Then Call**
- Send a LinkedIn connection request with a short note
- Once connected, send a voice note (LinkedIn Premium feature) — surprisingly high response rate
- After they view your profile (check Sales Navigator viewer alerts), follow up with a call the next day
