## KEY INSURANCE THEMES ACROSS THE REGION

### 1. GPU Transit / Marine Cargo
The region is receiving **hundreds of thousands of high-value Nvidia GPUs** in phased shipments (HUMAIN's 600K, G42's 60K+ pipeline, etc.). Each GB300 chip costs $30K–$40K. Transit insurance for these consignments is a major near-term opportunity. Marine + air cargo coverage for chips traveling from TSMC (Taiwan) → US → Middle East.

### 2. Data Center Physical Asset / Property
- Saudi Arabia: 61 data centers; UAE: 57
- Regional data center capacity set to triple from 1 GW to 3.3 GW by 2030
- New builds in extreme climate environments (heat, humidity) create novel engineering risks
- NEOM Oxagon: seawater cooling system — novel risk

### 3. Cyber Liability
All major operators (G42/Core42, HUMAIN, SDAIA, TII, Qai) are developing:
- National AI cloud platforms
- Arabic LLMs with government data
- Critical infrastructure AI integration
Nation-state attack surface is significant; sovereign AI is a geopolitical target.

### 4. Export Control / Regulatory Liability
US export controls on advanced GPUs (A100, H100, B200, GB300) to UAE and Saudi Arabia were contentious through 2024-2025. Entities navigating export licensing face compliance risk. US Commerce approved 70,000 GB300 chips for UAE/Saudi Arabia in Nov 2025 — but the regime remains dynamic.

### 5. D&O / Management Liability
Most sovereign AI entities employ Western (particularly American) executives in senior roles:
- Peng Xiao (G42) — Chinese-American
- Tareq Amin (HUMAIN) — Jordanian-American
- International research faculty at MBZUAI, TII
These executives face dual-jurisdiction liability exposure (US + GCC).

### 6. Business Interruption
Entities like HUMAIN and SDAIA are positioning their GPU infrastructure as **national critical infrastructure**. Outages affecting government AI services create BI scenarios beyond simple hardware replacement.

### 7. Construction / Erection All-Risks
- Stargate UAE (1 GW, Abu Dhabi)
- DataVolt / NEOM Oxagon (1.5 GW, Tabuk)
- Humain AI factories (multiple locations, Saudi Arabia)
- Qai Integrated Compute Center (Doha)
