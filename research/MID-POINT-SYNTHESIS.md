## SECTION 2: MOST INTERESTING / UNEXPECTED FINDINGS

### 2.1 The CoreWeave Debt Load Is Bigger Than the Entire Insurance Market Expected

CoreWeave's S-1 (March 2025) disclosed **$14.6B in technology equipment on the balance sheet**, financed by **$12.4B+ in debt**. The debt is structured across multiple SPVs (CoreWeave Compute Acquisition Co. IV, V, VI, VII, LLC) and perfected via UCC-1 filings listing collateral agents (U.S. Bank Trust Company) and secured parties (Blackstone, Magnetar, Coatue). This isn't speculative — it's public record, audited, and confirmed.

**Why this is unexpected:** Pre-S-1, CoreWeave's debt exposure was partially public via press releases but the balance sheet quantum wasn't visible. At $14.6B in equipment and $12.4B+ in debt, this is a company where equipment insurance isn't optional — it's contractually required by lenders. And yet no specialized GPU RVG product covers them.

**The implication for Corgi:** CoreWeave isn't just a customer — it's the *proof of concept* for the entire market. Their debt structure is the case study that sells the product to every other lender.

### 2.2 The Apollo/Valor/xAI "Triple Net Lease" — A New Asset Class Is Born

In January 2026, Apollo Global Management led a **$3.5B capital solution** for Valor Compute Infrastructure L.P. (VCI), a special purpose vehicle that acquired NVIDIA GB200 GPUs and leased them to xAI under a **triple net lease**. NVIDIA itself invested as an anchor LP in VCI. This is structurally identical to how aircraft are financed in the ABS market.

**Why this is unexpected:** GPU triple-net-lease structures didn't exist at scale until this deal. It's the aircraft-leasing model applied to semiconductors — which means residual value insurance on the underlying asset (the GPU) is the logical next product, exactly as it is in aviation. The Apollo/VCI deal is not just a financing event; it's the opening of a new asset class. Corgi's RVG is the missing insurance layer for that asset class.

**The ABS connection:** Lambda Labs issued the first GPU ABS in 2025 ($1.1B, AAA-rated), and the deal used a **40% residual value floor** as credit enhancement. The underwriters literally needed an RVG to make the deal work. They did it via a negotiated one-off guarantee — not because a product exists, but because they had to improvise. This is Corgi's most compelling early sales pitch to Lambda and future ABS issuers.

### 3.4 Data Point Gaps

**GPU Failure Rates — Thin Actuarial Data**
The NEWS-MONITORING file notes "GPU failure rates: 2–3% annually across large deployments" and flags liquid cooling CDU failures as a new risk vector. But this is a single source (introl.com). The actuarial model needs:
- Failure rates by GPU generation (H100 vs. A100 vs. V100)
- Failure rates by deployment environment (air-cooled vs. liquid-cooled)
- Mean time to repair vs. mean time to replace
- Replacement lead times (critical for BI calculation: Blackwell supply constrained through mid-2026)

The depreciation-models-academic.md and equipment-rvi-deep-dive.md files exist but weren't fully reviewed in this synthesis. These likely contain additional actuarial inputs.

**Secondary Market Pricing — Good Snapshot, Weak Trend Data**
The gpu-secondary-market.md has excellent point-in-time data (H100 used prices, A100 collapse, cloud rental price decline). What's missing is the *trend rate* — how quickly do GPU prices decline per quarter, and does the decline follow a predictable curve or is it discontinuous (shock depreciation at new generation release)?

This matters enormously for RVG pricing. An aircraft RVI insurer can use 20 years of Boeing 737 resale data. Corgi has 4 years of NVIDIA data at most. The actuarial model needs to be explicit about this uncertainty and priced accordingly (wider bands, shorter policy terms, lower guaranteed floors initially).

**UCC Filing Intelligence — Methodology Confirmed But Not Executed**
The enrichment/ucc-filings-gpu-intelligence.md file laid out exactly how to search Delaware UCC filings for GPU-backed debt. But the research didn't actually run the searches. The free intelligence described in the SYNTHESIS.md as "do it today" hasn't been done. This is actionable immediately at zero cost.
