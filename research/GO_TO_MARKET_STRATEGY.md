## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Corgi Insurance is entering a confirmed white-space market: **no standalone commercial GPU Residual Value Guarantee (RVG) insurance product exists for enterprise buyers.** The market fundamentals are extraordinary:

- **$14.3B+ in disclosed GPU-backed debt** (CoreWeave alone: $12.4B+)
- **$90–120B in GPU hardware sitting on corporate balance sheets** globally
- **GPU depreciation of 50–70% within 2 years** of each generation transition
- **Existing RVG pricing benchmark:** Tokio Marine HCC charges 4–7% of guaranteed amount for traditional assets
- **The first GPU ABS deal (2025) already uses a 40% residual value insurance floor** as credit enhancement
- **Amazon shortened server useful life** to 5 years in Feb 2025 — the only hyperscaler to do so — explicitly citing AI hardware obsolescence

The product is a **put option on GPU hardware, priced as insurance.** Borrowers buy it, lenders require it, and reinsurers back it. The three-sided value proposition:

| Stakeholder | Value |
|---|---|
| **GPU Operator** | Balance sheet protection; better financing terms; 150–200bps spread reduction on debt |
| **Lender** | Insured collateral; higher advance rates (40% → 70%+ LTV); standardized credit framework |
| **Reinsurer** | Novel, uncorrelated risk class; premium income on a rapidly growing asset base |

**Conservative market sizing:** At 5% penetration of $30B in financed GPU assets, with 4–7% premiums on 30% coverage → **$1.2–2.1B annual premium market.** Growing 30%+ annually with GPU capex.

**Target structure for first programme:**

| Parameter | Target |
|---|---|
| Capacity | $50–100M per risk |
| Coverage | 30–50% of GPU fleet fair market value at a future date (12–36 months) |
| Premium range | 4–7% of guaranteed amount (aligned to Tokio Marine HCC benchmarks) |
| Attachment | Per-fleet basis (not portfolio) initially |
| Carrier paper | Lloyd's syndicate (Beazley, CNA Hardy) or Bermuda (Tokio Marine HCC) |
| Programme structure | Delegated authority (MGA/coverholder) via Howden Tiger SabRE or direct |

**Critical decision: MGA vs. Coverholder vs. Direct.**
- **MGA route** (via Howden Tiger SabRE): Fastest to market. You underwrite on carrier paper with delegated authority. Requires MGA license.
- **Coverholder at Lloyd's**: Formal route if targeting Lloyd's syndicates. Takes 3–6 months for approval. Higher credibility.
- **Direct carrier partnership**: Simplest. Tokio Marine HCC or RVI Group adds GPU to their existing RVG product; you act as distribution/origination.

**Recommendation:** Start with a direct carrier partnership (Tokio Marine HCC extending their existing RVG product to GPUs) while pursuing MGA/coverholder status for medium-term independence.
